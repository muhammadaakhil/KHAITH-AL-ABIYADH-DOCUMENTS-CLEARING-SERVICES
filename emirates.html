Learn Emirates ID Services

Emirates ID Services refer to the services related to the issuance, renewal, and management of Emirates Identity Cards, commonly known as Emirates ID. Emirates ID is a crucial government-issued identification card for residents and citizens of the United Arab Emirates (UAE). These services are provided by the Emirates Identity Authority, now known as the Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship (ICA), in the UAE.

Individuals, including citizens and residents, must apply for an Emirates ID card. The application process typically involves submitting personal information and required documents, undergoing biometric data capture (including fingerprints and a facial photograph), and paying the associated fees. After the application is approved, the individual's Emirates ID card is issued. The card serves as an official proof of identity and includes key personal information, a photograph, and a unique identification number. Emirates ID cards have a specified validity period, after which they must be renewed. The renewal process usually includes updated biometric data capture and the issuance of a new card. In case of a lost, stolen, or damaged Emirates ID card, individuals can apply for a replacement card. This process requires reporting the loss or damage and submitting the necessary documents and fees.

If there are changes in an individual's personal information, such as a change of address or marital status, these updates can be made through Emirates ID Services. The ICA offers online services for individuals to track the status of their Emirates ID applications, renewals, and updates. It may also provide online appointment booking for in-person services. e-Kiosks are self-service machines located in various government and public places where individuals can apply for new Emirates ID cards, renew existing cards, and update their information. Emirates ID cards are commonly used for various smart services in the UAE, including electronic access to government and private-sector services, identification during healthcare visits, and for official transactions.
