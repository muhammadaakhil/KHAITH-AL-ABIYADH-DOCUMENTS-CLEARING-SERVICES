Learn Pakistan Passport Renewal and Smartcard Services

The Pakistan Passport Renewal and Smartcard Services refer to the services provided by the Directorate General of Immigration and Passports (DGIP) in Pakistan for the renewal of Pakistani passports and the issuance of Machine Readable Smart National Identity Cards (SNICs) commonly known as Smartcards. These services are essential for Pakistani citizens to ensure the validity of their travel documents and national identity cards.

Passport renewal is necessary for Pakistani citizens when their current passport is about to expire or has already expired. It ensures that individuals have a valid travel document to facilitate international travel. Pakistani citizens, including those residing in Pakistan and abroad, can apply for passport renewal. The passport renewal process typically involves submitting a passport renewal application, along with the required documents and fees, to the relevant authorities. The application is processed by the DGIP or its designated offices. Applicants usually need to provide their expiring/expired passport, a set of recent photographs, a copy of their Computerized National Identity Card (CNIC), and other supporting documents as per DGIP guidelines. The processing time for passport renewal can also vary, with expedited options available for those who require their passports more quickly.

The Smart National Identity Card (SNIC), also known as a Smartcard, serves as the official national identity document for Pakistani citizens. It contains biometric information and is used for various purposes, including voting in elections, accessing government services, and identification. All eligible Pakistani citizens can apply for a Smart National Identity Card once they reach the required age. To obtain a Smartcard, individuals need to apply to the National Database and Registration Authority (NADRA), which is responsible for issuing these cards. The application involves providing biometric data and other required documents. Applicants are typically required to provide their CNIC, proof of residence, photographs, and other necessary documentation as specified by NADRA. Smartcards have an expiry date, and individuals need to renew their cards when they expire.
